Albert Einstein

The Father of Modern Physics

Albert Einstein, photographed in 1921 after winning the Nobel Prize in Physics.

Here's a timeline of Albert Einstein's life:

  • 1879 - Born in Ulm, Kingdom of Württemberg, German Empire
  • 1901 - Gains Swiss citizenship and graduates from the Swiss Federal Polytechnic School in Zürich
  • 1905 - Publishes his "Annus Mirabilis" papers, including the famous E = mc² equation
  • 1915 - Completes the general theory of relativity
  • 1921 - Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics for his services to Theoretical Physics
  • 1933 - Emigrates to the United States due to the rise of Nazi Germany
  • 1939 - Writes a letter to President Roosevelt, warning about the potential development of "extremely powerful bombs of a new type"
  • 1955 - Dies in Princeton, New Jersey, at the age of 76

"The important thing is not to stop questioning. Curiosity has its own reason for existing."
